Reasoning in Human and Machines

Main Project at UChciago

  • Large Language Models
  • fMRI
  • Computational modelling
  • Naturalisitic experiments

Reasoning is a hallmark of human intelligence. And it's a million-dollar question both in the field of cognitive science and computer science. During my PhD, I aim to use novel behavioral tasks, namly interactive detective novels, along with sota computational models, imaging methods to look into this problem.

On one hand, I will work with cognitive/neural scientists to understand how large-scale dynamics in brain support the complex reasoning dynamics, and how attention and memory in humans contribute to this process. On the other hand, I will work with computer scientists to use transformer-based models as modal animals to see what are the commonalities and differences between them and humans. Hopefully, this will give us some insight into the computational mechanism behind reasoning.

More is on the way... Stay Tuned!
