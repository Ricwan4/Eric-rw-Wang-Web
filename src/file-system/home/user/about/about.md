



# Hi there

My name is Eric (Rui-Wen) Wang, a cognitive neuroscientist. Currently, I'm a Neubauer PhD Fellow at the University of Chicago working with Monica Rosenberg. My research focuses on reasoning dynamics across human and machine intelligence. Sepcifically, I use a combination of behavioral experiments (both naturalistic and controlled), neuroimaging and modeling to understand how humans and machines solve complex reasoning problems, and how we can use insights to build better AI systems and understand the human mind.


Before coming to the windy city, I completed my undergraduate degree at the Chinese University of Hong Kong in Psychology where I developed a line of research on eye movement strategy modeling. Then, I moved to University of Macau as a fMRI research assistant. Since there are too many Erics, and too many Wang et al. So I use Eric Rui-Wen Wang (E.R.W.W or Wang E.R et al.) to publish my papers.
